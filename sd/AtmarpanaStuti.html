Atmarpana Stuti


आत्मार्पणस्तुतिः
Srimad Appayya Dikshitendra, a renowned exponent of Advaita, lived from 1520 to 1593 AD. Among his many works is the deeply moving "AtmArpaNastuti". Legend has it that he consumed a datura seed to test his devotion, leading to the creation of this beautiful hymn in praise of Lord Shiva. In "AtmArpaNastuti", he extols Lord Shiva as the supreme deity, though it's essential to note that as an Advaitin, he saw no distinction between Shiva and Vishnu, despite his apparent focus on Shiva.

कस्ते बोद्धुं प्रभवति परं देवदेव प्रभावं
यस्मादित्थं विविधरचना सृष्टिरेषा बभूव ।
भक्तिग्राह्यस् त्वमिह तदपि त्वामहं भक्तिमात्रात्
स्तोतुं वाञ्छाम्यतिमहदिदं साहसं मे सहस्व ॥ १॥

क्षित्यादिना मवयववतां निश्चितं जन्म तावत्
तन्नास्त्येव क्वचन कलितं कर्त्रधिष्ठान हीनम् ।
नाधिष्ठातुं प्रभवति जडो नाप्यनीशश्च भावः
तस्मा दाद्यस् त्वमसि जगतां नाथ जाने विधाता ॥ २॥

इन्द्रं मित्रं वरुण मनिलं पद्मजं विष्णुमीशं
प्राहुस्ते ते परमशिव ते मायया मोहितास् त्वाम् ।
एतैः साकं सकलमपि यच्छक्तिलेशे समाप्तं
स त्वं देवः श्रुतिषु विदितः शम्भुरित्यादिदेवः ॥ ३॥

आनन्दाद्यः किमपि च घनीभाव मास्थाय रूपं
शक्त्या सार्धं परम मुमया शाश्वतं भोग मृच्छन् ।
अध्वातीते शुचि दिवसकृत् कोटि दीप्त्रे कपर्दिन्
आद्ये स्थाने विहरसि सदा सेव्यमानो गणेशैः ॥ ४॥

Assuming an indescribable oceanic solidity, adorned with Uma, the Lord with matted hair plays eternally in the primordial realm, radiant like a million suns, served by the lords of the gaNas.


त्वं वेदान्तैः विविधमहिमा गीयसे विश्वनेतः
त्वं विप्राद्यैर् वरद निखिलै रिज्यसे कर्मभिः स्वैः ।
त्वं दृष्टानुश्रविक विषया नन्द मात्रावि तृष्णै:
अन्तर्ग्रन्थि प्रविलय कृते चिन्त्यसे योगि वृन्दैः ॥ ५॥

O Lord of the Universe, your countless glories resonate in the Upanishads. Bestower of blessings, you are revered by Brahmanas and others through their prescribed rites. Yogis, disillusioned with worldly pleasures, meditate on you for liberation from all bondage.


ध्यायन्तस् त्वां कतिचन भवं दुस्तरं निस्तरन्ति
त्वत् पादाब्जं विधिवदितरे नित्य माराधयन्तः ।
अन्ये वर्णाश्रम विधिरताः पालयन्तस् त्वदाज्ञां
सर्वं हित्वा भवजलनिधा देश मज्जामि घोरे ॥ ६॥

Some transcend this cycle of birth and death through meditation on You, others by devoutly worshiping Your lotus feet as prescribed in scriptures, and yet some by following the duties of their varNa and Ashrama. Failing to pursue any of these paths, I find myself submerged in the relentless ocean of worldly existence.

18-May-2024

उत्पद्यापि स्मरहर महत् युत्तमानां कुलेऽस्मिन्
आस्वाद्य त्वन् महिम जलधे रप्यहं शीकराणून् ।
त्वत् पादार्चा विमुख हृदयश् चापलादिन्द्रियाणां
व्यग्रस् तुच्छेष् वहह जननं व्यर्थयाम्येष पापः ॥ ७॥

O Destroyer of Kama! Even though I was born in a great lineage and have learned a little about Your glories, my mind is still not devoted to worshipping Your feet. My senses are restless, and because of this, I am wasting my life on trivial things, making my birth pointless.


अर्कद्रोण प्रभृति कुसुमै रर्चनं ते विधेयं
प्राप्यं तेन स्मरहर फलं मोक्ष साम्राज्य लक्ष्मीः ।
एतज्जानन्नपि शिव शिव व्यर्थयन् काल मात्मन्
आत्मद्रोही करण विवशो भूयसाधः पतामि ॥ ८॥

O Destroyer of Kama! Even with simple wild flowers like erukkam poo and thumbap poo, worshipping You can lead to supreme bliss and liberation. Despite knowing this, I am wasting my time, controlled by my senses, and falling lower, O supreme Self!


किं वा कुर्वे विषम विषय स्वैरिणा वैरिणाहं
बद्धः स्वामिन् वपुषि हृदय ग्रन्थिना सार्ध मस्मिन् ।
उक्ष्णा दर्प ज्वरभर जुषा साक मेकत्र बद्धः
श्राम्यन् वत्सः स्मरहर युगे धावता किं करोतु ॥ ९॥

What can I do, O Lord? In this body, I am bound by uncontrollable sense objects and deep-seated desires. How can a calf, despite its best efforts, keep up with a wildly running bull when they are yoked together, O Slayer of Kama?


नाहं रोद्धुं करण निचयं दुर्नयं पारयामि
स्मारं स्मारं जनिपथरुजं नाथ सीदामि भीत्या ।
किं वा कुर्वे किमुचित मिह क्वाद्य गच्छामि हन्त
त्वत् पादाब्ज प्रपतनमृते नैव पश्याम्युपायम् ॥ १०॥

I cannot control my wayward senses. Thinking repeatedly about the sufferings of this cycle of rebirth, I tremble with fear, O Lord! What should I do? What is the right thing to do? Where shall I go now? Alas! I see no other way but to totally surrender at Your lotus feet.


उल्लङ्घ्याज्ञा मुडुपति कलाचूड ते विश्व वन्द्य
त्यक्ताचारः पशुव दधुना मुक्तलज्जश् चरामि ।
एवं नानाविध भवतति प्राप्त दीर्घापराधः
क्लेशाम्भोधिं कथ महमृते त्वत् प्रसादात्तरेयम् ॥ ११॥

O Lord, wearing the crescent moon on Your forehead and worshipped by the whole world! I have ignored Your commands and abandoned good conduct, now living shamelessly like an animal. Having committed many transgressions in this mundane life, how can I cross this ocean of sorrow without Your grace?


क्षाम्यस् येव त्वमिह करुणा सागरः कृत्स्नमागः
संसारोत्थं गिरिश सभय प्रार्थना दैन्य मात्रात् ।
यद्यप्येवं प्रतिकल महं व्यक्तमागः सहस्रं
कुर्वन् मूर्खः कथमिव तथा निस्त्रपः प्रार्थयेयम् ॥ १२॥

O Lord Girisha, ocean of compassion, You will surely forgive all sins born from this cycle of rebirth if one sincerely prays for forgiveness in fear. Yet, despite knowing this, I continue to commit thousands of sins every moment. Overwhelmed by shame and feeling speechless, how can I pray shamelessly?


सर्वं क्षेप्तुं प्रभवति जनः संसृति प्राप्तमागः
चेतः श्वास प्रशम समये त्वत् पादाब्जे निधाय ।
तस्मिन् काले यदि मम मनो नाथ दोषत्रयार्तं
प्रज्ञाहीनं पुरहर भवेत् तत्कथं मे घटेत ॥ १३॥

One can be freed from all sins incurred during worldly life by fixing the mind on Your lotus feet at the moment of death. But if, at that time, my mind is afflicted by the three evils (those from my body, surroundings, and acts of God) and loses consciousness, O Destroyer of the three cities, how will I be able to focus on Your lotus feet?


प्राणोत्क्रान्ति व्यतिकर दलत् सन्धिबन्धे शरीरे
प्रेमावेश प्रसर दमिता क्रन्दिते बन्धुवर्गे ।
अन्तः प्रज्ञा मपि शिव भजन्नन्तरायै रनन्तैः
आविद्धोऽहं त्वयि कथ मिमा मर्पयिष्यामि बुद्धिम् ॥ १४॥

At the time life leaves my body, when all my limbs are weak and my relatives cry uncontrollably out of affection for me, even if I am conscious, O Lord, how can I concentrate my mind on You amidst so many distractions?

अद्यैव त्वत् पद नलिनयो रर्पयाम्यन्त रात्मन्
आत्मानं मे सह परिकरै रद्रिकन्याधि नाथ ।
नाहं बोद्धुं शिव तव पदं न क्रिया योगचर्याः
कर्तुं शक्नोम् यनितरगतिः केवलं त्वां प्रपद्ये ॥ १५॥

O Indwelling Self! I surrender myself and my descendants at Your lotus feet right now, O Lord of the Mountain's Daughter! I cannot understand Your true nature of Existence, Consciousness, and Bliss, nor can I perform Vedic rituals or yoga practices. Having no other means, I seek refuge in You alone.


यः स्रष्टारं निखिल जगतां निर्ममे पूर्वमीशः
तस्मै वेदा नदित सकलान् यश्च साकं पुराणैः ।
तं त्वामाद्यं गुरु मह मसा वात्म बुद्धि प्रकाशं
संसारार्तः शरण मधुना पार्वतीशं प्रपद्ये ॥ १६॥

Tormented by this cycle of rebirth, I now seek refuge in You, the Lord who first created Brahma, the Creator of all worlds, and imparted the Vedas and Puranas to him. You are the Lord of Goddess Parvati, the primordial Guru who grants self-knowledge.


ब्रह्मादीन् यः स्मरहर पशून् मोह पाशेन बद्ध्वा
सर्वा नेकश् चिद चिद धिकः कारयित्वाऽऽत् मकृत्यम् ।
यश्चैतेषु स्वपद शरणान् विद्यया मोचयित्वा
सान्द्रानन्दं गमयति परं धाम तं त्वां प्रपद्ये ॥ १७॥

I surrender myself to You, O Destroyer of Desire. You are the One beyond all living and non-living things. You have bound all beings, from Brahma downwards, with the rope of illusion and made them act according to Your will. You free those who seek refuge in You by granting them self-knowledge, leading them to supreme bliss.


भक्ताग्र्याणां कथमपि परैर् योऽचिकित्स्या ममर्त्यैः
संसाराख्यां शमयति रुजं स्वात्मबो धौषधेन ।
तं सर्वाधीश्वर भव महा दीर्घ तीव्रामयेन
क्लिष्टोऽहं त्वां वरद शरणं यामि संसार वैद्यम् ॥ १८॥

O Mighty Lord! None of the other gods can relieve even the greatest devotees from the cycle of rebirth. Only You can cure this affliction with the remedy of Self-knowledge. Suffering from the severe ailment of endless worldly existence, I seek refuge in You, the healer of this affliction.


ध्यातो यत्नाद्विजित करणैर् योगिभिर्यो विमुक्त्यै
तेभ्यः प्राणोत् क्रमण समये संनिधा यात्मनैव ।
तद् व्याचष्टे भवभयहरं तारकं ब्रह्म देवः
तं सेवेऽहं गिरिश सततं ब्रह्मविद्या गुरुं त्वाम् ॥ १९॥

O Girisha! I adore You, the teacher who teaches the ultimate knowledge of Brahman. Yogis, who have mastered their senses, seek You earnestly. At the moment of death, You personally appear and give the liberating mantra, freeing us from the fear of rebirth.


दासोऽस् मीति त्वयि शिव मया नित्यसिद्धं निवेद्यं
जानास् येतत् त्वमपि यदहं निर्गतिः संभ्रमामि ।
नास्त्ये वान्यन् मम किमपि ते नाथ विज्ञापनीयं
कारुण्यान् मे शरणवरणं दीनवृत्तेर् गृहाण ॥ २०॥

I acknowledge before You, O Shiva, that I am Your devoted servant. You are aware of my despair and lack of options. I have nothing else to offer You, O Lord. Please, out of Your compassion, accept the surrender of this wretched soul.


ब्रह्मोपेन्द्र प्रभृति भिरपि स्वेप्सित प्रार्थनाय
स्वामिन्नग्रे चिर मवसरस् तोषयद्भिः प्रतीक्ष्यः ।
द्रागेव त्वां यदिह शरणं प्रार्थये कीट कल्पः
तद् विश्वा धीश्वर तव कृपामेव विश्वस्य दीने ॥ २१॥

O Lord, even Brahma, Vishnu, and other deities must wait patiently before You, seeking a chance to fulfill their desires. I, as insignificant as a worm, seek swift refuge in You solely due to my faith in Your boundless compassion, O Lord of the universe.


कर्म ज्ञान प्रचय मखिलं दुष्करं नाथ पश्यन्
पापासक्तं हृदय मपि चापारयन् सन्निरोद्धुम् ।
संसाराख्ये पुरहर महत्यन्ध कूपे विषीदन्
हस्तालम्ब प्रपतन मिदं प्राप्य ते निर्भयोऽस्मि॥ २२॥

O Lord, realizing the challenges of the paths of action and knowledge, and unable to restrain my mind drawn towards wrongdoing, suffering in the vast abyss of worldly existence, I now find solace in surrendering myself into Your comforting embrace, O Lord Shiva, free from fear.

त्वामे वैकं हतजनिपथे पान्थ मस्मिन् प्रपञ्चे
मत्वा जन्म प्रचय जलधेः बिभ्यतः पारशून्यात् ।
यत्ते धन्याः सुरवर मुखं दक्षिणं संश्रयन्ति
क्लिष्टं घोरे चिरमिह भवे तेन मां पाहि नित्यम् ॥ २३॥

Frightened by the endless cycle of births and suffering, I have long endured intense pain. Knowing You are my sole companion in this world of relentless suffering from repeated births and deaths, I implore You, O greatest of gods, to always protect me with Your face turned southward, where the fortunate sages seek refuge.


एकोऽसि त्वं शिव जनिमता मीश्वरो बन्ध मुक्त्योः
क्लेशाङ्गा रावलिषु लुठतः का गतिस् त्वां विना मे ।
तस्मादस्मिन् निह पशुपते घोरजन्म प्रवाहे
खिन्नं दैन्या कर मतिभयं मां भजस्व प्रपन्नम् ॥ २४॥

O Shiva, You alone control the bondage and liberation of those subject to birth. What refuge do I have besides You, as I wallow in the embers of suffering? Therefore, O Pashupati, save me from this terrible torrent of transmigratory existence, the most dreadful repository of all misery.


यो देवानां प्रथम मशुभद्रावको भक्ति भाजां
पूर्वं विश्वाधिक शत धृतिं जाय मानं महर्षिः ।
दृष्ट्यापश्यत् सकल जगती सृष्टि सामर्थ्य दात्र्या
स त्वं ग्रन्थि प्रविलयकृते विद्यया योज यास्मान् ॥ २५॥

O Lord beyond this universe, foremost among gods in eliminating the suffering of devotees, great seer whose glance created the universe through Brahma at his birth, please be kind enough to grant me knowledge to loosen the knot of bondage.


यद्याकाशं शुभद मनुजाश् चर्मवद् वेष्टयेयुः
दुःखस् यान्तं तदपि पुरुषस् त्वामविज्ञाय नैति ।
विज्ञानं च त्वयि शिव ऋते त्वत् प्रसादान् न लभ्यं
तद्दुःखार्तः कमिह शरणं यामि देवं त्वदन्यम् ॥ २६॥

O Bestower of good! Even if men could roll up the sky like a sheet of leather, they would not end all sorrow without knowing You. Knowledge of You cannot be attained without Your grace. Overcome by sorrow, which god can I seek refuge in other than You?


किं गूढार्थै रकृत कवचो गुम्फनैः किं पुराणैः
तन्त्राद्यैर्वा पुरुष मतिभिर् दुर्निरूप्यैकमत्यैः ।
किं वा शास्त्रै रफल कलहोल्लास मात्र प्रधानैः
विद्या विद्येश्वर कृतधियां केवलं त्वत् प्रसादात् ॥ २७॥

What is the use of the collection of words that are uncreated by anyone (apauruSheya) and whose meaning is hidden (the Vedas), or the Puranas, or the Tantra, which are difficult for human intelligence to understand? What is the use of the Shastras that indulge only in futile disputes? For those whose minds are fixed on You, knowledge is attainable by Your grace alone, O Lord of knowledge.


पापिष्टोऽहं विषय चपलः सन्तत द्रोहशाली
कार्पण्यैक स्थिर निवसतिः पुण्य गन्धा नभिज्ञः ।
यद्यप्येवं तदपि शरणं त्वत् पदाब्जं प्रपन्नं
नैनं दीनं स्मरहर तवोपेक्षितुं नाथ युक्तम् ॥ २८॥

I am the worst among sinners, fickle-minded towards sense-objects, always harming myself by identifying with my body and mind, the very abode of all weaknesses, and ignorant of even the scent of meritorious actions. Even though this is so, it is not proper for You to reject this miserable one who has surrendered himself at Your lotus feet, O Lord, Destroyer of Cupid.


आलोच्यैवं यदि मयि भवान् नाथ दोषाननन्तान्
अस्मत् पादाश्रयण पदवीं नार्हतीति क्षिपेन् माम् ।
अद्यैवेमं शरण विरहाद् विद्धि भीत्यैव नष्टं
ग्रामो गृह्णात् यहित तनयं किं नु मात्रा निरस्तम् ॥ २९॥

O Lord, if You reject me due to my innumerable faults, saying I do not deserve refuge at Your feet, then know that I am already lost out of fear of having no protector. Will the village accept a wayward son who has been rejected by his own mother?


क्षन्तव्यं वा निखिलमपि मे भूत भावि व्यलीकं
दुर्व्यापार प्रवण मथवा शिक्षणीयं मनो मे ।
न त्वेवार्त्त्या निरति शयया त्वत् पदाब्जं प्रपन्नं
त्वद् विन्यस् ताखिल भरममुं युक्तमीश प्रहातुम् ॥ ३०॥

Either forgive all my past and future sins, or instruct my mind, which is inclined towards evil actions, to proceed in the right way. It is not proper for You, O Lord, to reject one who has surrendered at Your lotus feet in unsurpassed anguish and who has placed all his burden on You.

सर्वज्ञस्त्वं निरवधि कृपासागरः पूर्ण शक्तिः
कस्मादेनं न गणयसि मा मापदब्धौ निमग्नम् ।
एकं पापात्मकमपि रुजा सर्वतोऽ त्यन्तदीनं
जन्तुं यद् युद्धरसि शिव कस् तावताति प्रसङ्गः ॥ ३१॥

You are omniscient, an ocean of boundless compassion and infinite power. Why do you not consider me, who is immersed in the ocean of suffering? If You uplift one creature, even though a sinner, who is intensely miserable due to suffering, how would that be too much, O Shiva?


अत्यन्तार्ति व्यथित मगतिं देव मामुद्धरेति
क्षुण्णो मार्गस् तव शिव पुरा केन वाऽनाथ नाथ ।
कामालम्बे बत तदधिकां प्रार्थनारीति मन्यां
त्रायस् वैनं सपदि कृपया वस्तु तत्त्वं विचिन्त्य ॥ ३२॥

O Lord, has anyone made unavailable the path of surrender to You in the form of "uplift me who am tormented by extreme suffering and am without any recourse," O Protector of the helpless! What method of prayer greater than this can I adopt? Considering the actual state of things, save me immediately by Your compassion. The path of total surrender is stressed as superior to the other paths.


एतावन्तं भ्रमण निचयं प्रापितोऽयं वराकः
श्रान्तः स्वामिन् नगति रधुना मोचनीयस् त्वयाहम् ।
कृत्याकृत्य व्यपगत मतिर् दीन शाखामृगोऽयं
संताड्यैनं दशन विवृतिं पश्यतस्ते फलं किम् ॥ ३३॥

This miserable one has been subjected to wandering through innumerable births for so long. I am tired and without any means of escape, O Lord. Now I should be released by You. What do You gain by beating this miserable monkey who does not know what should and should not be done, and seeing him baring his teeth?


माता तातः सुत इति समाबध्य मां मोहपाशै:
आपात्यैवं भवजलनिधौ हा किमीश त्वयाऽऽप्तम् ।
एतावन्तं समय मियती मार्ति मापादि तेऽस्मिन्
कल्याणी ते किमिति न कृपा कापि मे भाग्यरेखा ॥ ३४॥

O Lord, what have You gained by binding me with the rope of delusion to mother, father, son, and others, and throwing me into the ocean of transmigratory existence? Why has Your benevolent compassion not fallen on me, who have been suffering for so long? It is only my fate.


भुङ्क्षे गुप्तं बत सुखनिधिं तात साधारणं त्वं
भिक्षावृत्तिं परमभिनयन् मायया मां विभज्य ।
मर्यादायाः सकल जगतां नायकः स्थापकस् त्वं
युक्तं किं तद्वद विभजनं योजयस् वात्मना माम् ॥ ३५॥

O Father, you secretly enjoy the treasure of bliss that is the common property of both of us, assuming the role of a mendicant and separating me by Maya. You, the maker and controller of the rules of contact for the whole world, say whether such a separation of me is proper. Unite me with You.


न त्वा जन्म प्रलय जलधे रुद्धरा मीति चेद्धीः
आस्तां तन्मे भवतु च जनिर् यत्र कुत्रापि जातौ ।
त्वद् भक्ताना मनितर सुखैः पादधूली किशोरैः
आरब्धं मे भवतु भगवन् भावि सर्वं शरीरम् ॥ ३६॥

If You decide not to lift me out of the ocean of repeated births, then let me be born as any creature. But may all my future bodies be blessed by the incomparable bliss of contact with the dust of the feet of Your devotees.

कीटा नागास् तरव इति वा किं न सन्ति स्थलेषु
त्वत् पादाम्भोरुह परिमलोद् वाहिमन्दा निलेषु ।
तेष्वेकं वा सृज पुनरिमं नाथ दीनार्त्ति हारिन्
आतोषान्मां मृड भव महाङ्गार नद्यां लुठन्तम् ॥ ३७॥

Worms, mountains, trees, and what else are not there in the places where the gentle breeze brings in the fragrance of Your lotus feet! Make me, who is wallowing in this river of the embers of samsara, one of these (again and again) until You are satisfied. O Lord Shiva, Remover of the suffering of the weak.


काले कण्ठ स्फुरद सुकलालेश सत्तावलोक-
व्याग्रोदग्र व्यसनि सकल स्निग्ध रुद्धोप कण्ठे ।
अन्तस्तोदै रवधि रहिता मार्ति मापद्य मानेपि
अङिघ्र द्वन्द्वे तव निविशता मन्तरात्मन् ममात्मा ॥ ३८॥

O Indwelling Self of all! When, at the time of my death, I am suffering countless internal pangs and the throats of those around me are choked by grief upon seeing my breathing becoming faint, let my self be united with Your two feet (let me attain liberation).

अन्तर् बाष्पाकुलित नयना नन्तरङ्गा नपश्यन्
अग्रे घोषं रुदित बहुलं कातराणा मश्रुण्वन् ।
अत्युत्क्रान्ति श्रम मगणयन् अन्त काले कपर्दिन्
अङ्घ्रिद्वन्द्वे तव निविशता मन्तरात्मन् ममात्मा ॥ ३९॥

O One with matted hair! At the final moment of my life, without seeing my loved ones in sorrow, without hearing the cries of those in fear, and without feeling the pain of departing life, let my self merge into Your feet.
(The poet prays that at the time of his death, his mind should be so completely absorbed in the Lord that he does not see, hear, or feel anything else.)


चारुस्मेरानन सरसिजं चन्द्र रेखावतंसं
फुल्लन् मल्ली कुसुम कलिका दाम सौभाग्य चोरम् ।
अन्तः पश्याम् यचल सुतया रत्नपीठे निषण्णं
लोकातीतं सततशिवदं रूप मप्राकृतं ते ॥ ४०॥

I see in my mind Your supreme form, with Your lotus face adorned by a charming smile and the crescent moon on Your forehead, surpassing the beauty of a garland of jasmine buds. Seated on a throne of gems with the Daughter of the Mountain, You remain beyond the worlds, ever bestowing auspiciousness.


स्वप्ने वापि स्वरस विकसद् दिव्य पङ्केरुहाभं
पश्येयं किं तव पशुपते पादयुग्मं कदाचित् ।
क्वाहं पापः क्व तव चरणालोक भाग्यं तथापि
प्रत्याशां मे घटयति पुनर् विश्रुता तेऽनुकम्पा ॥ ४१॥

O Pashupati, may I one day see in my dreams Your two feet, shining with the splendor of fully bloomed lotuses! Where am I, a sinner, and where is the good fortune to see Your feet? Yet, Your well-known compassion gives me this hope.


भिक्षावृत्तिं चर पितृवने भूतसङ्घैर् भ्रमेदं
विज्ञातं ते चरित मखिलं विप्रलिप्सोः कपालिन् ।
आवैकुण्ठ द्रुहिण मखिल प्राणिना मीश्वरस् त्वं
नाथ स्वप्नेऽ प्यह मिह न ते पादपद्मं त्यजामि ॥ ४२॥

O Kapalin! Whether You act as a mendicant on the cremation ground or roam about with the Bhutas, Your entire conduct, which seems to mislead, is well known. You are the Lord of all beings, from Vishnu and Brahma downwards. O Lord, I will not forsake Your lotus feet even in my dreams.

आलेपनं भसित मावसथः श्मशानं
अस्थीनि ते सतत माभरणानि सन्तु ।
निह्नोतु मीश सकल श्रुति पार सिद्धं
ऐश्वर्य मम्बुज भवोऽपि च न क्षमस्ते ॥ ४३॥

Let it be that Your unguent is only ash, Your abode is the cremation ground, and Your ornaments are always bones. But, O Lord, even Lord Brahma cannot deny Your lordliness, which is affirmed by all the shrutis.


विहर पितृवने वा विश्वपारे पुरे वा
रजतगिरि तटे वा रत्नसानु स्थले वा ।
दिश भवदुपकण्ठं देहि मे भृत्यभावं
परमशिव तव श्री पादुका वाहकानाम् ॥ ४४॥

O Paramashiva! Whether in the cremation ground, in an abode beyond the universe, on the Silver Mountain (Kailasa), or in the valley of gems, make me the servant of those who carry Your sacred sandals, and bless me with proximity to You.


विविधमपि गुणौघं वेदयन् त्यर्थवादाः
परिमित विभवानां पामराणां सुराणाम् ।
तनु हिमकर मौले तावता त्वत् परत्वे
कति कति जगदीशाः कल्पिता नो भवेयुः ॥ ४५॥

Let the arthavAdAs (statements of praise) in the Vedas attribute various kinds of merits to other gods who possess limited power and glory. O Lord with the crescent moon on Your forehead! In this way, many lords of the world are mistakenly imagined to be superior to You!


बल मबल ममीषां बल्बजानां विचिन्त्यं
कथमपि शिव काल क्षेप मात्र प्रधानैः ।
निखिलमपि रहस्यं नाथ निष्कृष्य साक्षात्
सरसिज भव मुख्यैः साधितं नः प्रमाणम् ॥ ४६॥

The strength or weakness of these weak ones (the various gods) may be a matter of discussion among those whose objective is only to spend time, O Lord Shiva! For us, what has been established by Brahma and others after delving into the secrets of the scriptures is what is valid.

न किंचिन् मेनेऽतः समभिलषणीयं त्रिभुवने
सुखं वा दुःखं वा मम भवतु यद्भावि भगवन् ।
समुन्मीलत् पाथोरुह कुहर सौभाग्य मुषिते
पद द्वन्द्वे चेतः परिचय मुपेयान् मम सदा ॥ ४७॥

I do not consider anything in the three worlds to be worthy of being desired. O Bhagavan, let me have happiness or sorrow as destined. Let my mind always rest on Your two feet, which excel the splendor of the inside of a lotus in full bloom.


उदरभरण मात्रं साध्य मुद्दिश्य नीचेषु
असकृ दुपनिबद्धा माहितोच्छिष्ट भावाम् ।
अहमिह नुतिभङ्गीम् अर्पयित् वोपहारं
तव चरण सरोजे तात जातोऽपराधी ॥ ४८॥

After repeatedly engaging in composing various works of a low order solely to earn my livelihood, I am now offering only the remnants of praise at Your lotus feet, and thus I have become guilty, O Father!


सर्वं सदाशिव सहस्व ममापराधं
मग्नं समुद्धर महत् यमुमापदब्धौ ।
सर्वात्मना तव पदाम्बुज मेव दीनः
स्वामिन् ननन्य शरणः शरणं प्रपद्ये ॥ ४९॥

O Sadashiva, forgive all my transgressions. Uplift this one submerged in the ocean of suffering. Helpless and with no other refuge, I surrender at Your lotus feet alone with all my heart, O Lord.


आत्मार्पण स्तुति रियं भगवन् निबद्धा
यद्यप् यनन्य मनसा न मया तथापि ।
वाचापि केवल मयं शरणं वृणीते
दीनो वराक इति रक्ष कृपानिधे माम् ॥ ५०॥

O Bhagavan, though this AtmArpaNastuti has been composed by me without my mind being totally concentrated on You, protect me, O Abode of Compassion, considering that this helpless, miserable one is surrendering to You at least by words.


इति श्रीमत् अप्पय्य दीक्षितेन्द्राणां कृतिष् वन्यतमा आत्मार्पण स्तुतिः सम्पूर्णा ।
